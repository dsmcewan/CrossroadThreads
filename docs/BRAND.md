# Crossroad Threads — Brand Concept

> The complete Crossroad Threads concept, as recorded from the founder (2026-07-01).
> This is the source of truth for voice, wings, and brand rules. The catalog
> (`content/designs.json`) must conform to it.

## Thesis

Southern Gothic Americana meets mythology — every design pulls from Delta blues, Route 66, Southern Gothic imagery, and global myth as if they all stopped at the same haunted crossroad. Wearable mythology; each piece reads like a relic or roadside flyer. A publishing house that prints on cotton.

## Aesthetic

Woodcut/letterpress style, tan-black-cream (plus rust red, bone white, deep indigo from the original spec). Vintage poster/tour-shirt formats. Symbols over spectacle.

## Brand rules (locked)

- **The shirt is the wink, the placard can explain.** Explanation lives in the museum layer, never on the garment.
- **Word golf**: reward the viewer who knows the reference, don't punish the one who doesn't.
- Within a series, the frame repeats (the "bless her heart" opener), but **punchline structure must vary** — imperative, image, question — so wings read curated, not templated.
- **Don't chase topical references** (the memecoin stays out of the Tulips design; 1637 doesn't expire).
- **No likenesses, no copyrighted lyrics** (Hatshepsut's Lion King line killed for cotton).

## Collections / Wings

**Founding document:** Robert Johnson "Saved" — the crossroads origin piece, serious enough for the Mississippi Blues Museum pitch.

**Bless Your Heart (Badass Women of History):** Boudica, Grace O'Malley, Olga of Kiev, Ching Shih ("More Boats More Problems"), Tomoe Gozen ("Beautiful. Deadly. Recorded."), Hatshepsut ("The Bearded Woman"), Rani of Jhansi ("Child and Sword"), Artemis ("No Peeking" — Actaeon in two words).

**Cautionary Tales:** Medusa ("Look Away, Darling"), Get Wrecked With a Siren, Date a Silkie ("They Always Leave the Shore"), Nizari ("Sweet Dreams, Saladin"), Decimation ("9/10 Play Again"), Rudis, plus the Speculation cluster — Tulips ("Buy the Dip"), The Spanish ("Drowned in Silver"), Weighing of the Heart (production note: feather down, heart up, or the joke inverts). Tabled: Artemisia I of Caria (no slogan landed). Reserved: "To the Moon" for a different bubble.

**Fake Business Format:** Charon Charters ("Always 2 Cents" — River Styx / Crossroads / Bayou Landing), Persephone Café, "Never Trust the Fae" (Bayou Ballroom, Route 68, "Keep Salt and Iron Handy" — the world-building anchor piece).

**Princess Treatment:** Lady Jane Grey's 9 Day Juice Cleanse and kin — purpose-built for Tudor exhibition gift shops. Origin: the princess conversation with the founder's daughter; her rebellion shirt proved the concept.

**Pantheon / Myth Series:** Aphrodite ("Seafoam Beauty Flirts With War"), Persephone, Djinn, Pandora ("Check Your Box, Pandora" — comma intentional, vocative clapback), Dionysus Diners & Dives (god in the glass, Flavortown-coded classical elements, no Fieri likeness).

Format is the asset. **Corporate Jargon** and the **tarot register** (Artemis tarot card) exist as named lines with less locked inventory.

## Channel strategy

Placard copy is dual-purpose: Shopify product pages and museum-buyer credibility. Target eight to ten institutions whose collections match specific designs before touching the Museum Store Association route. Women's cuts identified as a real opportunity — the catalog skews toward the historically literate woman most history apparel ignores.
